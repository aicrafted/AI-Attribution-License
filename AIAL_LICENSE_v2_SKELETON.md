# AI-Attribution License (AIAL) v2 — Skeleton

## 0. Preamble (non-operative)
This License is intended to provide a permissive grant for software distribution while allowing Contributors to make provenance declarations about Covered Contributions and, where expressly stated, to adopt a limited no-claim posture for certain declared generated-origin contributions.

**Drafting note:**  
Do not overstate legal certainty. Keep this modest.

---

## 1. Definitions

### 1.1 “Contributor”
A person or legal entity that distributes, publishes, or makes available a Covered Contribution under this License.

### 1.2 “Covered Contribution”
A file, generated artifact, release element, or other identified contribution distributed under this License by a Contributor.

### 1.3 “Recipient”
Any person or entity receiving a Covered Contribution under this License.

### 1.4 “Provenance Declaration”
A declaration made by a Contributor or Originator regarding the stated provenance of a Covered Contribution.

### 1.5 “Originator”
The person or entity making the Provenance Declaration for a Covered Contribution.

### 1.6 “Generated-Origin Declaration”
A Provenance Declaration stating that the Contributor or Originator designates the Covered Contribution as `generated-origin` under the accompanying provenance specification or equivalent declaration.

## 4. Provenance Declarations

A Contributor may provide Provenance Declarations for Covered Contributions.

Provenance Declarations:
- are informational representations by the declaring party,
- do not by themselves determine the legal status of the Covered Contribution,
- do not negate third-party rights,
- do not expand permissions beyond rights held by the declaring Contributor.

**Drafting note:**  
This section is critical.

## 5. Optional No-Claim / Covenant for Declared Generated-Origin Contributions

Where a Contributor expressly designates a Covered Contribution as subject to an additional no-claim declaration, the Contributor states that, to the extent the Contributor holds any copyright or similar exclusive rights in that Covered Contribution, the Contributor:

- does not assert such rights against Recipients exercising permissions granted under this License; and
- covenants not to assert such rights against such exercise.

If any such disclaimer, waiver, or covenant is ineffective under applicable law, the Contributor grants the broadest additional permission permitted by applicable law to achieve substantially the same practical result.

**Drafting note:**  
This is the replacement for “public domain because fully AI-generated”.

## 6. No Inference from Silence or Ambiguity

The absence, ambiguity, incompleteness, or staleness of any Provenance Declaration:
- does not reduce ordinary legal protections,
- does not imply that a Covered Contribution is generated-origin,
- does not imply that a no-claim posture applies,
- does not affect third-party or unknown rights.

- supersede separate licenses applicable to imported, inherited, or vendored code.
